## 🚨 Traffic Penalties

Understanding how penalties work is crucial for every driver. Penalties are not just fines — they can include **penalty points**, **license suspension**, and in some cases even **criminal charges** depending on how serious the violation is.  

### 💸 Fines Based on Speeding

Romanian traffic law uses a *penalty point system* that directly influences the fine amount:

- Exceeding speed limit by **10–20 km/h** → moderate fine + 2–3 penalty points  
- Exceeding speed limit by **21–30 km/h** → higher fine + 4–5 points  
- Exceeding speed limit by **31–40 km/h** → significant fine + 6–8 points  
- Exceeding speed limit by **41–50 km/h** → large fine + 9–20 points  
- Over **50 km/h excess** → major fine + license suspension for 90 days:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

💡 Each penalty point is worth a percentage of the minimum wage, so fines can range from hundreds to thousands of lei depending on how much the limit was exceeded.:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## 📉 License Suspension

Romanian authorities take repeated or serious violations seriously:

- **License suspension can occur for serious offenses**, such as excessive speeding or dangerous overtaking.:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  
- If you reach **15 penalty points within a period**, the license may be suspended automatically.:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  
- Suspensions may range from **30 to 90 days** or more for aggressive or repeated offenses.:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

This system ensures that **repeat offenders face harsher consequences**, not just financial, but also losing their right to drive.

## 📌 Why Penalties Matter

Penalties aren’t just punishment — they’re designed to:  

- Reduce dangerous behaviors  
- Deter repeat offences  
- Encourage safer roads for everyone

Always obey posted limits and rules to avoid penalties **and help make roads safer** for all drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
